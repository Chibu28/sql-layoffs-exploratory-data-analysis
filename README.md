Global Layoffs SQL Exploratory Data Analysis

Project Overview

This project explores global layoff trends using SQL to uncover patterns across industries, companies, geographic regions, funding stages, and time periods. The objective was to identify the sectors and organisations most affected by workforce reductions and generate business insights from the data.



Tools Used

* SQL (MySQL)
* MySQL Workbench
  


Business Questions

The analysis aimed to answer the following questions:

* Which companies experienced the largest layoffs?
* Which industries were most affected by layoffs?
* Which companies had the highest cumulative layoffs?
* Were there industries that avoided complete shutdowns?
* What relationship exists between funding levels and company survival?
* Which countries and locations were most impacted?
* How did layoff trends change over time?
* Which company funding stages recorded the highest layoffs?


Key Findings

Largest Layoff Event

Google recorded the largest single layoff event in the dataset, laying off approximately 12,000 employees in 2023. The company belonged to the Consumer industry, highlighting the significant impact experienced within this sector.

Industries Most Affected

The Consumer industry recorded the highest total layoffs, followed by the Other and Retail industries.

Companies with the Highest Total Layoffs

Across the entire timeline, the companies with the highest cumulative layoffs included:

* Amazon
* Google
* Meta
* Salesforce
* Microsoft

A notable observation is that these organisations are all large, well-established companies, suggesting that workforce reductions were not limited to smaller firms or startups.

Industry Resilience

Out of the 33 industries represented in the dataset, the following industries experienced no instances of complete workforce reductions (100% layoffs):

* Sales
* Hardware
* Energy
* Manufacturing
* Fintech
* Crypto

This may indicate greater operational resilience or more stable business models within these sectors.

Funding and Company Survival

Companies that experienced complete workforce reductions generally raised lower levels of funding. However, the existence of similarly funded companies that remained operational suggests that funding alone does not determine business survival.

Geographic Distribution of Layoffs

The United States accounted for the largest share of layoffs, reflecting its concentration of major technology firms and startups. Despite this dominance, layoffs were observed globally, indicating that workforce reductions were part of a broader international trend.

Yearly Layoff Trends

Layoffs fluctuated significantly over time:

| Year | Total Layoffs |
| ---- | ------------- |
| 2020 | ~81,000       |
| 2021 | ~15,800       |
| 2022 | ~161,000      |
| 2023 | ~127,000      |

The sharp increase observed during 2022 and 2023 suggests a period of widespread organisational restructuring.

Funding Stage Analysis

Companies in the **Post-IPO stage** recorded the highest number of layoffs. As publicly traded companies are often under pressure to maintain profitability and meet shareholder expectations, they may be more likely to implement large-scale workforce reductions during periods of economic uncertainty.

Companies Leading Layoffs by Year

Examples of companies with the highest layoffs in specific years include:

2020: Uber and Booking.com
2021: ByteDance and Katerra

These findings demonstrate that layoff activity shifted across organisations over time rather than being concentrated within a single group of companies.
